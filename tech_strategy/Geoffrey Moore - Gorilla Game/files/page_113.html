It takes a pair of CAP charts to show the full extent of how the market corrects the aging gorilla's market capitalization, and it is not a pretty sight.
Figure 4.19
The Death of a Gorilla, Part 1: Competitive Advantage Consumed
This is the first of the two charts. It positions the upcoming conversion to the new paradigm as an immovable object blocking future returns from the existing category at some point in the future. This means that CAP cannot grow any further to the right. But time marches on, so every year the area under the curve is diminished. The shaded rectangle on the left represents the loss in valuation just in the first year of this process. Since the area under the curve equals valuation, this means the stock price of the company is irrevocably hammered. Worse, since the market can anticipate this entire future in the present, it eliminates even the possibility of upside from its calculation
