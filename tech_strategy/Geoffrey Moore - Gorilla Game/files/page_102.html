The result was an upside surprise, not in earnings, but in perceived competitive advantage (which, of course, is expected to turn into improved earnings going forward). When this happens in a market dominated by an aging gorilla, it simply pumps more life into the old fellow's body. It does not, however, represent a shift in advantage the way a tornado does, and therefore the ratio does not leap up the way it will in the next case we look at. But it does give the stock price a good shot in the arm, because extending the competitive advantage period increases the net present value of the company.
