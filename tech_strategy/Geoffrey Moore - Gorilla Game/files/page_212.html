Arguably, a gorilla gamer could have bought a few months sooner, at the Cisco IPO. It is hard to suggest, however, that anyone following our rules would have needed more evidence to buy Cisco. But this is typical of the gorilla game. A six-month window is a far different call to action than a six-hour or six-day window. Gorilla games move to the rhythm of quarterly reports, not stock tickers.
