Rule 9. In a gorilla collision, hold your gorilla candidates until there has been a definitive outcome.
This was the case that we discussed under Rule 4, where at the beginning of 1994 a potential collision between routers and intelligent hubs was further complicated by the introduction of LAN switching into the market. It had a disastrous local impact on all the stocks, but we held on until we could see a definitive outcome.
From time to time we have flirted with an alternative rule here, which would be to sell out the category until the smoke clears and then buy back in. At the end of the day, however, the signals for when to sell and then buy seemed so murky that we gave it up.
