Time passes, and both Oracle and RTI continue to grow, but the latter grows at a much more modest rate. At this point you should be combing the trade press looking for reasons to get out of RTI, and if you had not reacted before, you would once you read the headline in the July 24, 1989 Computerworld: "RTI Shuffles into No. 2 spot." Gorilla-game status does not get any plainer than that, and looking at the size differential between RTI and Oracle, and the ongoing lament about RTI's inability to market itself effectively, you would have sold on the July closing price, if not before.
