Some Background on Cisco
The 1980s was the decade of the PC, and towards its latter half it saw a huge rise in local area networks. For the most part, however, LANs were simply workgroups looped together by wire to share expensive peripherals, like a laser printer, and to share files. All that changed, however, with the advent of electronic mail.
Electronic mail, and all the extensions to it that have followed, including the World Wide Web, have driven meteoric escalations in demand for interconnecting computers. These connections have proliferated at every level of organizationfrom workgroup to workgroup, department to department, enterprise to enterprise, home to office, hotel to office, consumer to vendor, country to country, citizen to citizen. They have made the 1990s the decade of inter-networking, be it expanding outward from the LAN to the WAN (wide are network), or exploding inward, converting what used to be a simple hierarchy of LANs into a segmented and switched fabric that rivals the telephone system in its complexity.
