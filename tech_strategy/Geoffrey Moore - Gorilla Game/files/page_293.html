Investing in the InternetThe Mother of All Tornadoes?
To close this book, we want to take you on a test drive of gorillagame methodology, and given that we are entering the fourth quarter of 1997 at the time of this writing, as we look out to the technology stock market, we see, like Michael Jordan, "nothing but Net."
The Internet itself is not so much in or out of the tornado as it is a tornado alley, a place of recurring tornadoes, and therefore a great place for staking out a tornado watch. In this chapter we will identify a series of actual and potential tornado opportunitiessome early, some late, some ripe for the momentand suggest how the gorilla game looks to be playing out within each case.
We will, in a word, go on record.
The thought is chilling. What is more unpredictable than the stock market? And what in the stock market is more unpredictable than high tech? So why would any sane person put predictions in a book? What, then, would the National Enquirer be for?
